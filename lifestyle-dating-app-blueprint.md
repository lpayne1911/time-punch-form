# Product Blueprint: A Discreet, Compliant Dating & Community App for Adult Lifestyle Audiences

**Working codename:** *Velvet* (see naming options in §1)
**Document type:** Full product, compliance, trust-and-safety, and monetization blueprint
**Audience served:** Adults interested in alternative relationship dynamics, kink-aware dating, ethical non-monogamy, consent culture, and lifestyle compatibility
**Core constraint:** Must pass Apple App Store and Google Play review and stay approved long-term.

> **Read this first.** The single biggest mistake teams make in this category is designing the product they *want* (an uncensored FetLife successor) and then trying to "make it compliant" with a thin marketing layer. That fails review and gets pulled. This blueprint inverts that: it designs a genuinely consent-first, privacy-first, moderated dating product whose *true nature* is store-compliant. The discretion serves real adult users; it is not a disguise for prohibited activity. If you build the prohibited product behind a compliant skin, no amount of copywriting saves you. Build the compliant product.

## 1. Product Concept and Positioning

### Refined product thesis
A private, members-style dating and community platform where verified adults connect through **compatibility of values, boundaries, communication style, and relationship intentions** — not explicit browsing. It serves people exploring or living alternative relationship structures who are poorly served by mainstream dating apps (which flatten them) and by legacy adult communities (which feel unsafe, unmoderated, and impossible to list on app stores).

### App category
**Lifestyle / Social / Dating.** Positioned as a *premium private social and dating club* for consenting adults. Not "Entertainment," not anything adult-content-coded.

### Target users
Verified adults (18+/19+ where required) who want intentional connection around relationship style, communication, and lifestyle compatibility — singles, couples, and ethically non-monogamous people. (Full breakdown in §3.)

### Primary user problems
- Mainstream apps don't let them express relationship structure or boundaries without stigma or bans.
- Legacy communities feel unsafe, poorly moderated, image-heavy, and expose their identity.
- Hard to verify that the people they meet are real, adult, and respectful of consent.
- Privacy fear: being discovered by coworkers, family, or contacts.
- No trusted place to learn norms (consent, communication, negotiation) as a newcomer.

### Emotional promise
*"You can be your full self, safely, with people who get it — without exposure, judgment, or pressure."* Belonging + discretion + respect.

### What the app **is**
A consent-centered, privacy-first dating and community app for adults that matches on compatibility, supports education, hosts verified real-world and online community events, and is human-moderated.

### What the app **is not**
Not pornography. Not a hookup marketplace. Not an explicit-content platform. Not an escort/sugar/companionship marketplace. Not a solicitation tool. Not anonymous random chat. Not a place to buy or sell sexual services or media.

### Store-compliant description
> "A private, members-first dating and community app for verified adults seeking meaningful, intentional connection based on shared values, communication styles, and relationship compatibility. Built around consent, privacy, and safety."

### How to avoid looking like an explicit adult-content platform
- No media feed, no public galleries, no "discover by photo wall."
- Profiles are text-and-compatibility-forward; photos are limited, moderated, and blurred until mutual match.
- Interest taxonomy uses neutral, non-explicit vocabulary (see §13).
- No nudity, ever — enforced by automated + human moderation.
- Visual brand reads like a wellness/members-club app, not an adult site.

### How to appeal to lifestyle users without explicit language
Speak to *dynamics, structure, and intention* rather than acts: "communication style," "relationship structure," "roles & dynamics," "boundaries," "experience level," "what you're exploring." Insiders recognize the framing; reviewers see a values-based dating app. The signal is in *specificity and respect*, not explicitness.

## 2. Compliance-First Product Strategy

**Governing rule:** Every feature must survive the question *"Could a reviewer reasonably read this as facilitating pornography, prostitution, or unmoderated explicit UGC?"* If yes, redesign it.

| Major product decision | Why it supports compliance | How it reduces risk |
|---|---|---|
| No media feed; text/compatibility-forward profiles | Removes the "explicit content platform" read | No surface for porn UGC |
| Photos blurred until mutual match + moderated | Prevents browsing-for-bodies and nudity exposure | Cuts nudity, harassment, screenshot abuse |
| Neutral interest taxonomy (no explicit terms) | Listing & in-app text stay clean | Lowers "sexual content" classification |
| Mutual-match-gated messaging | No solicitation broadcast | Blocks spam, escort outreach |
| Verified adults + age assurance | Meets 18+ and child-safety expectations | Trafficking/minor risk reduction |
| Human + automated moderation, fast SLAs | Demonstrates UGC governance Apple/Google require | Removes "unmoderated" rejection basis |
| Monetization = privacy/discovery/convenience only | No "paid sexual access" read | Avoids prostitution-facilitation flag |
| Events are educational/social, host-verified, approved | No explicit event boards | Avoids real-world solicitation liability |
| Clear reporting/blocking/deletion | Required platform safety primitives | Direct rejection-avoidance |

**What reviewers might misunderstand, and how to prevent it:**
- *"This is FetLife/an adult app."* → Reviewer notes (§16) + clean listing + neutral first-run screens + no explicit terms anywhere in the review build. Seed the demo account with wholesome, compliant content.
- *"Lifestyle = sexual services."* → Make the consent pledge, community standards, and no-solicitation policy visible early in onboarding; surface moderation tooling in the demo.
- *"Photos = nudity risk."* → Show the blur-until-match and photo rules; provide the moderation dashboard walkthrough in reviewer notes.
- *"Events = hookups for pay."* → Show host verification + approval flow; categories are education/social only.

## 3. Target Audience

- **Primary:** Verified adults seeking intentional connection within alternative relationship dynamics; value privacy, consent, and quality over volume.
- **Secondary:** Curious-but-new adults exploring ethical non-monogamy or communication-forward dating; want a safe, educational on-ramp.
- **Premium:** Privacy-maximalists and high-intent daters who pay for incognito, verified-only browsing, travel mode, advanced filters, and event access.
- **Safety-conscious users:** People burned by unsafe platforms; want verification, reporting, check-ins, blocking that actually works.
- **Discreet users:** Professionals, public-facing people; need maximal privacy, hide-from-contacts, discreet notifications.
- **Newcomers:** Need education, gentle defaults, norm-setting, low-pressure discovery.
- **Experienced lifestyle users:** Want depth (boundaries, dynamics, structure fields), verified community, real events.
- **Couples / singles / ENM:** Profiles support solo, linked-partner, and structure disclosure; matching respects structure compatibility and consent of all linked parties.

**Do NOT target:** Minors (hard-blocked), people seeking paid sexual services, sellers of sexual content/companionship, traffickers/recruiters, those seeking non-consensual or coercive activity, escort advertisers.

**Actively discourage / ban:** Solicitation (buying or selling sex), commercial sexual content, off-platform "services" recruiting, coercion/harassment, impersonation, minors, financial scams, doxxing.

## 4. Core Product Principles
1. **Consent first** — every interaction is opt-in; pledge required; consent reminders in-product.
2. **Privacy by default** — most-private settings are the defaults.
3. **Adult-only access** — age assurance gates entry; no public surfaces.
4. **Safety before growth** — ship safety primitives before any growth lever.
5. **Discretion before virality** — no viral/social-graph mechanics that expose users.
6. **Moderation before scale** — capacity to moderate gates user-cap increases.
7. **Compatibility over swiping volume** — depth-based matching, daily curated sets, no infinite explicit wall.
8. **Trust over anonymity** — verification rewarded; pseudonyms allowed but accountable.
9. **Respectful discovery over explicit browsing** — no body-first browsing.
10. **Community standards over anything-goes.**
11. **No monetization resembling sexual access.**
12. **Zero tolerance** for coercion, harassment, exploitation, trafficking, non-consensual behavior.

## 5. Feature Design

- **Adult-only onboarding:** Age gate → adult-only notice → consent pledge → standards → privacy defaults, before any browsing.
- **Age assurance / verification:** Self-attested DOB gate + escalating assurance (device signals, optional third-party age estimation / ID check via provider for higher trust tiers and event access).
- **Identity verification options:** Selfie-liveness match to profile photo (basic); optional government-ID verification (premium/host/event tiers) via a provider — ID data tokenized, not stored raw.
- **Verification badges:** *Adult-Verified*, *Photo-Verified*, *ID-Verified*, *Host-Verified*. Visible, non-explicit.
- **Consent pledge:** Short, signed commitment to consent, respect, no solicitation, no minors — re-affirmed periodically.
- **Community standards agreement:** Plain-language rules acceptance, versioned.
- **Privacy-first profile setup:** Minimal required fields; everything else optional; private by default.
- **Discreet profile modes:** Standard / Private (visible only to mutual interests or verified) / Paused.
- **Profile visibility controls:** Per-field visibility; verified-only; hide-from-contacts; incognito.
- **Blurred/limited discovery until mutual match:** Photos blurred; limited fields shown pre-match.
- **Lifestyle interest tags (non-explicit):** Curated controlled vocabulary (see §13) — no free-text explicit fields.
- **Relationship intention tags:** e.g., "exploring," "long-term dynamic," "friendship & community," "structured relationship."
- **Boundary tags:** "Hard limits respected," "communication required," "slow to meet," "discretion essential."
- **Communication preference tags:** Direct / thoughtful / frequent / async-friendly.
- **Compatibility prompts:** Curated prompts about values, communication, boundaries.
- **Values-based & interest-based matching:** Weighted compatibility (see §10).
- **Verified-only browsing:** Filter to verified members (premium).
- **Incognito browsing:** Browse without appearing in others' "viewed me."
- **Travel mode:** Set a future/away city; privacy-protected.
- **Private circles:** Invite/approval-gated topical communities, moderated.
- **Event discovery:** Verified-host, approved, education/social events.
- **Educational resources & consent education:** Articles, workshops on communication, negotiation, safety.
- **Safety center:** Reporting, blocking, check-ins, meeting-safety guides, emergency pause.
- **Messaging after mutual interest** + **message request filters** + optional **read receipts (premium)**.
- **Blocking, reporting, user notes** (private notes on connections).
- **Safety check-ins:** Optional "let a friend know" / timed check-in around meetups.
- **Emergency profile pause:** One tap hides profile and pauses messaging.
- **Account deletion, data export, data minimization** — first-class, easy.
- **Moderation dashboard & admin review tools** (§36).

## 9. Profile Design (Store-Compliant Structure)
**Fields:** Display name (pseudonym allowed) · Age · General location (city/region, never precise) · Relationship status (optional) · Relationship intentions · Communication style · Lifestyle interests (controlled vocab) · Boundaries · "Looking for" · Experience level · Values · Availability (general, not for-hire) · Event interest · Verification status · Privacy preferences.

**Photo rules:** No nudity, no explicit content, no contact info/handles in images, must include a clothed face photo for verification tier; photos auto-screened + human-reviewed; blurred until mutual match. **Private album:** optional, *non-explicit only*, access by explicit per-person grant, access-logged, same moderation rules — never a paywalled explicit unlock.

**Profile prompts (examples):** "Communication matters to me because…" · "A boundary I always honor…" · "What I'm exploring right now…" · "What respect looks like to me…"

**Not allowed in profiles:** Explicit descriptions, solicitation, prices/rates, off-platform contact handles, "services," sugar/arrangement language, anything implying minors, hate speech, others' personal info (doxxing), nudity.

## 10. Matching System (Conceptual)
A weighted compatibility score, **not** appearance ranking:
- Lifestyle compatibility (shared/complementary interests)
- Relationship-intention compatibility
- Communication-style compatibility
- Boundary compatibility (respect alignment; hard-limit conflicts down-weighted)
- Location proximity (coarse)
- Verification level (trust boost)
- Privacy-preference alignment
- Activity level (recency, responsiveness)
- Mutual interest (two-sided)
- Dealbreakers (hard filters)
- **Safety signals** (report history, spam/risk scores) negatively weighted; risky accounts suppressed

**Avoiding appearance-only matching:** Photos are blurred pre-match and contribute nothing to score; compatibility prompts and tags drive recommendations. **Avoiding feeling ranked/objectified:** No public desirability score, no "hot/elite" tiers, no leaderboards; users see *why* someone is compatible ("shares your values on communication"), not a rating. Curated daily sets, not an infinite swipe wall.

## 11. Messaging System (Safe by Design)
- Messaging **only after mutual match**.
- Message requests gated; **verified-only filter** (premium).
- Intro prompts to start respectfully.
- Consent reminder on first message of a thread.
- Harassment + spam detection (text classifiers), image moderation on any sent media, link controls (warn/strip risky links, block known solicitation domains).
- Report and block **from the thread**; unmatch.
- Optional read receipts (premium); discreet notifications.
- Screenshot deterrence where technically feasible (flag/warn; cannot fully prevent — communicate honestly).
- Safety nudges before meeting (meet public, tell a friend, check-in).
- **Prohibited & auto-actioned:** explicit solicitation, illegal services, coercion, threats, blackmail, doxxing, minor-related content.

## 17. Trust and Safety System
- **Verification levels:** Unverified → Adult-Verified → Photo-Verified → ID-Verified → Host-Verified. Higher tiers unlock visibility/trust, not "access to people."
- **Age assurance:** gate + escalating signals + provider age estimation/ID for sensitive actions/events.
- **Profile, photo, private-album review:** automated screen + human queue; nudity/explicit auto-blocked.
- **Message-abuse detection:** harassment/threat/solicitation/scam classifiers; auto-flag + human review.
- **Report categories:** harassment, explicit content, solicitation/commercial sex, scam/financial, impersonation/catfish, threats/violence, doxxing, minor-safety, off-platform/unsafe, spam.
- **Enforcement ladder:** warning → content removal → temporary suspension → permanent ban; **immediate ban** for minors, trafficking, non-consensual content, threats, doxxing.
- **Appeals process:** in-app appeal, independent reviewer, defined timeline.
- **Law-enforcement escalation policy** + mandated child-safety reporting (e.g., NCMEC where applicable).
- **Policies:** anti-harassment, anti-coercion, anti-solicitation, anti-trafficking, anti-blackmail, anti-doxxing, anti-impersonation, **anti-minor (zero tolerance)**.
- **Consent & meeting & event safety guidance.**
- **Moderation SLAs:** urgent (minor-safety/threats) < 1h target; high (explicit/solicitation) < 12h; standard < 48h.
- **Safety-incident tracking:** every report logged, categorized, resolution-audited; transparency metrics.

## 18. Moderation Rules (Specifics)
- **Profiles:** no explicit text, prices, handles, solicitation, others' data, minors.
- **Photos / private albums:** no nudity/explicit/contact-info; album access logged; same standards as public.
- **Messages:** no solicitation, illegal services, coercion, threats, blackmail, doxxing; classifiers + human review.
- **Groups / circles:** moderated, host-accountable; no unmoderated groups.
- **Events / host pages:** approval before publish; education/social only; verified hosts; no explicit/illegal/paid-sexual content.
- **Reports:** triaged by category + SLA; reporter protected.
- **Repeat offenders:** escalating penalties; ban on threshold.
- **Off-platform solicitation:** warn/remove/ban; block known service domains/handles.
- **Explicit content / harassment / threats / coercion / commercial sexual services:** remove + escalate; ban for severe.
- **Fake accounts / spam / scams / catfishing / impersonation / financial manipulation / blackmail:** detection + ban; preserve evidence.
- **Minors:** instant removal, preserve evidence, report to authorities.
- **Unsafe meetups:** intervene with safety guidance; act on reports.

## 33. Payment Compliance
- **iOS digital subscriptions/features → Apple In-App Purchase.** **Android → Google Play Billing.** Required for in-app digital goods.
- **Do not** route users around store billing for in-app digital goods (no external links to pay for subscriptions inside the app, except where a jurisdiction's regulation explicitly permits and you follow the exact entitlement rules).
- Clear subscription terms; easy cancellation (deep-link to store management); no deceptive pricing; no hidden auto-renew; no manipulative paywalls.
- **Real-world event tickets** are physical/real-world goods/services and generally **may** use an external processor (Stripe) — both stores permit external payment for physical/real-world goods. Keep event ticketing UI and code clearly separated from digital-subscription flows so reviewers see the distinction.
- **Where external payments are appropriate:** real-world event tickets, host payouts. **Where they're not:** subscriptions, boosts, any in-app digital unlock.

## 35. Technical Architecture
- **iOS:** Swift / SwiftUI (or React Native / Flutter for cross-platform).
- **Android:** Kotlin / Jetpack Compose (or shared cross-platform).
- **Cross-platform option:** **Flutter** or **React Native** to share UI; native modules for secure media/verification.
- **Backend:** Node.js (NestJS) or Go; or Python (FastAPI). API: GraphQL or REST.
- **Database:** PostgreSQL (primary) + Redis (cache/sessions) + object storage (S3/GCS) for media; Elasticsearch/OpenSearch for discovery.
- **Auth:** OTP (phone/email) via Twilio/Firebase Auth/Auth0; JWT sessions; RBAC for admin.
- **Age verification providers:** Yoti, Veriff, Persona, Incode (age estimation + ID).
- **Identity verification:** Persona, Veriff, Onfido, Stripe Identity.
- **Image moderation:** AWS Rekognition / Google Cloud Vision / Hive (nudity/explicit detection) + human queue.
- **Text moderation:** Hive / OpenAI moderation / Perspective API + custom solicitation/scam classifiers.
- **Messaging infra:** Stream Chat / Sendbird, or custom WebSocket service.
- **Push:** APNs / FCM (generic discreet payloads).
- **Encryption:** TLS 1.2+; at-rest encryption (KMS); signed short-lived media URLs.
- **Secure media storage:** private buckets, no public ACLs.
- **Admin moderation dashboard:** internal web app (React) with RBAC + audit logging.
- **Analytics:** privacy-respecting (self-hosted or PostHog/Amplitude with sensitive-data exclusions).
- **Payments/subscriptions:** RevenueCat (manages Apple IAP + Play Billing + entitlements); Stripe Connect for events/payouts.
- **Event system:** custom + Stripe; **reporting system, audit logs, privacy tools, data-deletion workflow** as first-class services.

### Plain-English: why this can be compliant if built correctly
Apple and Google don't ban dating apps, adult (18+) audiences, or even apps for alternative-lifestyle communities. What they ban is **pornography, unmoderated explicit UGC, and the facilitation of prostitution/sexual services**. This product is engineered so its *actual behavior* avoids all three: there's no explicit-content surface to host porn, photos are moderated and blurred so it can't become a body-browsing wall, there's no marketplace or paid access to people so it can't facilitate sexual services, and there's real verification plus automated-and-human moderation with reporting, blocking, and deletion — exactly the governance the stores require for user-generated content. The discretion and privacy features serve legitimate adult users who fear exposure; they are not a cover for prohibited activity. Build the genuinely consent-first, privacy-first, well-moderated product described here — not a disguised version of a banned one — and it can pass review, stay approved, and monetize sustainably.
